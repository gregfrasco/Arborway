Welcome!

Welcome to The Arborway Committee for Public Transit, Inc. (ACPT). Organized by residents and merchants in Boston’s Jamaica Plain neighborhood in the 1970s, ACPT’s goal is to advocate for improved public transit service in Boston’s Jamaica Plain neighborhood and more generally throughout greater Boston. As a neighborhood organization we seek vibrant and inter-connective transit services. In doing so we work with other community organizations, our political representatives, and the business community to achieve these goals.

    • Our Current Major Transit Initiative is the .065-mile extension of the E-branch of the Green Line from its Heath Street, Jamaica Plain terminus along South Huntington Avenue to Hyde Square, Boston’s Latin Quarter, also in Jamaica Plain. The E-branch operates as both a light rail and streetcar service connecting northern Jamaica Plain into the Boston subway system. The importance of extending the service is underscored by the fact that Hyde Square is an environmental justice neighborhood lacking a rail connection to downtown Boston. In addition, in 2013, the Boston Planning and Redevelopment Agency (BPDA) designated South Huntington a “development corridor. ”Development has already attracted hundreds of new residents to the area with more to follow.
